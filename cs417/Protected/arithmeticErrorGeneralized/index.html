1 Comparing Arithmetic Error

Compare the arithmetic error between $x^{2} -yx$ and $x(x-y)$. Let us start by rewriting the finite precision version of each equation.

$$ \begin{eqnarray} x^{2} -yx &\phantom{10}vs\phantom{10}& x(x-y) \\ (x^{*})^{2} - y^{*}x^{*} &\phantom{10}vs\phantom{10}& x^{*}(x^{*}-y^{*}) \\ \end{eqnarray} $$

Let us tackle the equations one at a time.

1.1 Equation 1

$$ \begin{eqnarray} (x^{*})^{2} - y^{*}x^{*} &=& (x(1+\epsilon_{x})^{2} - y(1+\epsilon_{y})x(1+\epsilon_{x}) \\ &=& x^2 (1+\epsilon_{x})^{2} - xy(1+\epsilon_{x})(1+\epsilon_{y}) \\ &\approx& x^2 (1+2\epsilon_{x}) - xy(1+\epsilon_{x} + \epsilon_{y}) \\ &=& (x^2 -xy) + x^2 (2\epsilon_{x}) - xy(\epsilon_{x} + \epsilon_{y}) \\ \end{eqnarray} $$

That leads us to…

$$ abs. \phantom{1} error = \Big| x^2 (2\epsilon_{x}) - xy(\epsilon_{x} + \epsilon_{y}) \Big| $$

and

$$ rel. \phantom{1} error = \Big| \frac{x^2 (2\epsilon_{x}) - xy(\epsilon_{x} + \epsilon_{y})}{x^2 -xy} \Big| $$

1.2 Equation 2

$$ \begin{eqnarray} x^{*}(x^{*} - y^{*}) = &=& x(1+\epsilon_{x})(x(1+\epsilon_{x}) - y(1+\epsilon_{y})) \\ &=& x^2 (1+\epsilon_{x})^{2} - xy(1+\epsilon_{x})(1+\epsilon_{y}) \\ &\approx& x^2 (1+2\epsilon_{x}) - xy(1+\epsilon_{x} + \epsilon_{y}) \\ &=& (x^2 -xy) + x^2 (2\epsilon_{x}) - xy(\epsilon_{x} + \epsilon_{y}) \\ \end{eqnarray} $$

We can stop here. The expansions both lead to the same result…

$$ (x^2 -xy) + x^2 (2\epsilon_{x}) - xy(\epsilon_{x} + \epsilon_{y}) \\ $$

In this case… the relative errors, absolute errors, and expansions are the same.
