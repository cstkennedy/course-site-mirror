Two is Prime

Thomas J Kennedy

Task

Prove that 2 is the only even prime number.

Approach

Flip the question; show that every even number greater than 2 is composite (i.e., not prime).

1 Setting Things Up

Let us start by listing the first few prime numbers.

2 3 5 7 11 13 17 19 21

We know how to define a prime number:

For a number p to be prime, p must have exactly two factors, itself (p) and 1.

Let us list the first few even integers.

\begin{array}{ccccc} 2 & & 4 & & 6 & & 8 & & 10 \\ 2*1 & & 2*2 & & 2*3 & & 2*4 & & 2*5 \end{array}

Did you notice how every even number can be expressed as the product of 2 and another integer? Now we need to prove that all even numbers can be expressed as 2 times another number.

2 Proof by Induction

This is an excellent opportunity to review the First Principle of Mathematical Induction (or weak induction).

Claim

All even numbers can be represented by the product of 2 and another integer i, where i is the index.

Let us define:

$$ P(i) = 2i $$

Basis… and a little extra.

The first step is to show that $P(1)$ holds. I like to add a few extra “first steps” as a sanity check

\begin{array}{rl} P(1) &=& 2 \\ P(2) &=& 4 \\ P(3) &=& 6 \\ P(4) &=& 8 \\ \end{array}

Inductive Step

Now we will assume $P(n)$ holds, and prove $P(n+1)$.

\begin{array}{rl} P(n+1) &=& P(n) + 2 \\ 2(n+1) &=& 2n + 2 \end{array}

Now that we have applied the definition of $P$ to both sides, we must leave one side (the left) alone and focus on the other side (the right).

\begin{array}{rl} P(n+1) &=& P(n) + 2 \\ 2(n+1) &=& 2n + 2 \\ 2(n+1) &=& 2(n + 1) \\ \end{array}

Conclusion

All even numbers can be represented by $2 * n$.

$\forall_{p}$ where $ p > 2$, $n$ is not equal to $1$

$\therefore$ 2 is the only even prime number. Q.E.D.
