1 Reviewing

Everything up through the end of Module 9 (Object Oriented Programming) is covered by the Midterm Exam. Make sure that you have reviewed:

  • Module 5 - Design: Existing Implementation Perspective
  • Module 6 - Design Discussions (OOA&D) & Case Studies
  • Module 7 - (Pseudo-Module) Midterm (i.e., Midsemester) Review
  • Module 8 - Pointers & Sharing & Memory Management
  • Module 9 - Object Oriented Programming (OOP)

Make sure to emphasize the C++ Class Checklist review. At least three questions will focus on it. In fact… I would have it open alongside the Midterm Exam.

2 Types of Questions

The Midterm is composed of the following question types:

  • Multiple Selection - Select all that apply

  • Multiple Dropdown - Fill in multiple blanks from provided options

  • True/False - Select either true or false.

  • Multiple Choice - Select the one correct option.

  • Essay - Briefly discuss a concept (e.g., iterators) and/or the associated mechanics.

  • Short Answer - Fill in the blank with a few words (less than a sentence).

  • Essay - Write the appropriate function declarations (not definitions).

3 Taking the Exam

You will complete the Midterm Exam within Canvas. You can locate the link to the Midterm under the Exams link in the Canvas Navigation Menu.

3.1 When

You may complete the Midterm Exam during any contiguous 120-minute block on the specified dates.

3.2 Educational Accessibility - Extended Time

If you have have extended time through the Office of Educational Accessibility send me a message through Canvas no later than twelve (12) hours before the exam becomes available.

3.3 Proctoring

There is no proctor (or proctoring service) required for the exam. No lockdown-style browser is required. Access Canvas as you normally would.

3.4 Instructions

Before you begin the Midterm, you will be presented with instructions:

You are agreeing to abide by the following, in accordance with the ODU Honor Code:

All answers submitted must be your own work. You may not receive aid or information from anyone other than the instructor.

Printing or otherwise capturing any portion of this exam is forbidden.

You may not provide information to other students during the exam. You may not communicate with other students about the contents of the exam.

During the exam…

  1. You may refer to any notes or books that you have brought with you.

  2. You may use a web browser to visit the course web site… directly or through Canvas and any materials I have provided to you (e.g., Example Code).

  3. You may access any online resource with the exception of email and any resource that violates any rules established in this description.

You have 2 hours (120 minutes) to complete the exam. Note that thirty (30) of those minutes is intended as a buffer to mitigate (at least a little) the anxiety of a Midterm Exam.

3.5 Technical Issues

If you have any issues while taking the Midterm Exam… send me a message through Canvas as soon as the issue occurs.
